# NMR_Spectroscopy
Quantitative analysis of glyphosate using qNMR and structural elucidation of Aspirin using 1D/2D NMR (1H, 13C, DEPT, HSQC) and elemental analysis.
# Quantitative Analysis and Structural Identification via NMR Spectroscopy 

## Overview
This repository contains the methodology, spectral data, and final analysis for an experiment utilizing Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) spectroscopy. The project demonstrates the practical application of NMR for two critical analytical tasks: using quantitative NMR (qNMR) to determine the exact concentration of a target compound, and utilizing multi-dimensional NMR techniques to deduce the complete molecular structure of an unknown pharmaceutical sample.

## Objectives
* **Quantitative Analysis (qNMR):** Construct a robust calibration curve from known standards to precisely determine the concentration of an unknown glyphosate sample.
* **Structural Identification:** Interpret 1D (1H, 13C, DEPT) and 2D (HSQC) NMR spectra, alongside CHN elemental analysis, to characterize and map the chemical structure of an unknown organic compound.

## Tools & Technologies
* **Hardware:** SPINSOLVE 80 ULTRA Multi-X benchtop NMR spectrometer equipped with an autosampler.
* **Software:** SPINSOLVE and MNova for spectral processing and analysis; SciDAVis for data plotting and signal integration.

## Methodology & Key Findings

### 1. Glyphosate Quantification (qNMR)
To quantify the unknown sample, I utilized 31P NMR spectroscopy to analyze a series of glyphosate standards with concentrations ranging from 0.0479 M to 0.5636 M.
* Extracted and evaluated the integral areas of the diagnostic resonance signals for each standard.
* Generated a highly accurate linear calibration curve relating chemical concentration to signal area.
* **Result:** Successfully extrapolated the concentration of the unknown glyphosate sample to be 0.0468 M.

### 2. Unknown Compound Identification
For the second phase, I performed a comprehensive structural analysis on an unknown sample dissolved in CDCl3.
* **Elemental (CHN) Analysis:** Determined the sample was composed of 60% Carbon, 4.48% Hydrogen, and 35.52% Oxygen. This yielded the empirical formula C9H8O4 and a molar mass of 180.15 g/mol.
* **1H NMR:** Identified a carboxylic acid proton (10.5–13 ppm), four distinct aromatic protons indicating an ortho-disubstituted ring, and a methyl singlet (2.3 ppm).
* **13C & DEPT NMR:** Revealed two distinct quaternary carbonyl carbons and confirmed the presence of a methyl group, while ruling out any methylene (CH2) groups.
* **2D HSQC:** Mapped the carbon-hydrogen correlations, validating the backbone structure and confirming that all C-H bonds were properly accounted for.
* **Result:** By synthesizing the spectroscopic and elemental evidence, I conclusively identified the unknown compound as 2-acetoxybenzoic acid, widely known as Aspirin.

## Impact
This project showcases a robust, multi-dimensional approach to chemical quantification and structural elucidation. The ability to accurately process complex spectral data, cross-reference it with elemental analysis, and systematically deduce a molecular structure demonstrates a strong, hands-on foundation in analytical chemistry and advanced spectroscopy techniques.
